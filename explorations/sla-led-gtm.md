# SLA-led GTM — strategy sketch

A working-session sketch capturing four moves Joel is sitting with. None are settled. None should propagate to canonical docs until pressure-tested in a dedicated session.

## Moves under consideration

### 1. Launch with all nine message-library categories live

The current thinking is to drop the partial-coverage launch posture. Today MASTER_PLAN's "Launch focus" and the structural-patterns launch-posture model commit to four authored categories (Verification, Account events, Order updates, Team alerts) with the remaining five rendered as "Coming soon." This move would reverse that: every category authored before launch, no "Coming soon" tiles at all. Concretely it would mean front-loading the authoring passes for Appointments, Customer support, Marketing, Community, and Waitlist — work currently parked behind launch.

### 2. SLA tiering as the go-to-market spine

The current thinking is to lead with reliability as the marketable promise: a guaranteed high SLA for MakerKit first, extending to additional starter kits next, then steadily extending SLA guarantees to brownfield (existing-app) integrations as operational reliability is proven out. This is a new concept with no current home in the docs — neither MASTER_PLAN nor MARKETING_STRATEGY currently treats SLA as a tier or as the spine of the GTM story.

**Open question to flag:** "all categories live" (move 1) is a *capability* statement; the SLA tier is the actual *promise*. These two must not be conflated. Capability without a promise is feature coverage; promise without capability is unbacked marketing. Any propagation of this exploration would need to keep the two distinct on every surface.

### 3. Verification demoted from co-equal launch headline

The current thinking is that SMS OTP is losing ground to passkeys and TOTP, and RelayKit does not want to plant its identity on a weakening security primitive. So Verification would step back from its current position as one of two co-equal launch headlines.

**Open question to flag:** Verification is currently RelayKit's only fully horizontal category — it works across every vertical without specialization. Demoting it *as a use case* (i.e., reducing its presence in the product's offered surface area) is a distinct and unresolved choice from simply *not marketing* RelayKit as a security product. The first is a roadmap move; the second is a positioning move. This sketch does not resolve which is meant, and the two have very different downstream consequences.

### 4. Marketing positioning: offered, not identity

The current thinking is that Marketing-tier sends would still be offered, but explicitly not part of RelayKit's identity. RelayKit is not a marketing company. This would reverse the other half of the current "two co-equal launch features" framing (Verification + Marketing as the headline pair).

## Next
