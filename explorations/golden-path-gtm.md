# Golden-path-led GTM — strategy sketch

> **Rename note:** this file was `sla-led-gtm.md`. The "SLA-led" framing was found, in the working session that produced this rewrite, to mis-describe the actual thinking. SLA-as-a-marketed-promise is not the spine. The spine is the *golden path* — starter kits as the lowest-friction on-ramp. The old title is retired with the old framing.

A working-session sketch. Four moves are now substantially clarified from the original sketch. None are canon. They still need a dedicated pressure-test session before any D-number or MASTER_PLAN amendment.

---

## The one bet, and the three calls hanging off it

The original sketch listed four parallel "moves." That was wrong shape. There is **one bet** and **three positioning calls** that follow from it.

- **The bet (was Move 2):** RelayKit goes to market golden-path-first — starter kits as the on-ramp — not marketing-site-first.
- **Call A (was Move 1):** what "launch" means is redefined to match the bet.
- **Call B (was Move 3):** verification steps back from headline status.
- **Call C (was Move 4):** marketing steps back from headline status.

Calls A, B, and C are not independent debates. They are consequences of the bet. Pressure-test the bet first; the three calls resolve in its light.

---

## The bet — golden-path-led GTM

Starter kits are the spine of go-to-market: the lowest-friction, highest-success path to a working integration, and therefore the right place to build the first real customers.

**Not SLA-led.** The original sketch framed this as SLA tiering — a guaranteed reliability percentage, marketed as the promise. That framing is dropped. The thinking is not about an SLA % as a marketable headline. It is **strategic — about product development and distribution**: pick the path where a developer is most likely to succeed, build customers there, prove the motion, and learn from a controlled surface before widening. Reliability matters, but as something earned and demonstrated, not as a number printed on the marketing site.

**Starter kits as a distribution channel.** Starter kits are not just an easy integration target — they are a channel, with potential for agreements with their founders (co-marketing, endorsement, bundled documentation). That channel relationship is part of the bet, not a side effect.

### The trust-and-aperture curve

Golden-path-led GTM unfolds over time as a curve with two dials turned together: **who gets access** widens, and **how confidently RelayKit speaks about the product** rises — and the second only ever moves as far as operational reality backs it.

- **Stage 1 — One kit, locked.** One starter kit. Integration co-built, ideally with the founder's awareness (the agreement seam). Access is effectively invitation-grade. The marketing is *proof*, not reach — "this works, here is a real builder who did it." Reviews and comments stay positive because access is controlled. This is the ground to stand on and pound until it breaks.
- **Stage 2 — Two or three kits, still curated.** The same motion repeated. The point is not more users; it is proving the motion *generalizes* — that Stage 1 was not one lucky kit. Documentation hardens here: each new kit exposes what the docs silently assumed. Founder agreements mature into a real channel play.
- **Stage 3 — Open, but qualified.** Doors open to any developer, any starter kit, and (with caveats — see below) brownfield. The control that was a *human gate* in Stage 1 becomes an *editorial filter* in Stage 3: the documentation and the confidence level of the copy self-select capable developers. This is where category landing pages and SEO do their work, because inbound is now wanted.

**The gate relocates, it does not disappear.** Stage 1: the gate is human — RelayKit chooses who is in. Stage 3: the gate is editorial — the docs and the honest framing of the copy decide who succeeds. The whole journey is moving the gate from "us choosing" to "the writing doing the choosing." That move is only safe once the documentation and support capability are real — which is exactly what Stages 1–2 buy.

**Voice tracks the curve.** Stage 1: *evidenced* confidence — "here is the one that worked." Stage 2: *demonstrated* — "here is the pattern." Stage 3: *earned* — "here is the documented product, go." Confidence in the copy is dialed up only as far as operational reality backs it. This is the Voice Principles' "show, don't tell" applied to GTM.

### Two tensions to carry into the pressure-test (not resolved here)

1. **Brownfield is a different curve, not Stage 4.** Stages 1–3 across starter kits widen *one* controlled variable. Brownfield — arbitrary existing codebases, no clean starting state — is a different variable. It may not be the end of this curve; it may be its own curve that starts over. How much to "deal in" brownfield, and when, is explicitly TBD.
2. **Slow aperture has a cost.** Every month at invitation-grade is a month competitors are not gated. The curve buys trust and protects reviews, but it is not free. The tradeoff is a pressure-test question.

## Call A — "launch" redefined: the product, not the corpus

"Launch" has been ambiguous. The 9-category message library was completed and shipped as the **lead-magnet corpus**, and that shipped state got loosely called "launched." It is not the product launch.

**Launch is redefined as: the product is live — one starter kit, integrated end to end, a developer can go from clone to paid live texts.** Not "which message categories exist." The corpus is done; that question is closed. Launch now means the paid, integrated, working product on its first golden-path surface.

Consequence: the bet *is* the launch definition. There is no "launch the product, then also do starter kits." The starter kit is the product's first real surface. (This makes the original Move 1 — "all nine categories live" — already true and no longer a launch question. It also means the stale MASTER_PLAN "Launch focus" paragraph is not a small edit; the launch *definition* drifted, not just the "coming soon" list.)

---

## Call B — verification: offered, not identity

Verification (SMS OTP) steps back from headline status. SMS OTP is losing ground to passkeys and TOTP; RelayKit will not plant its brand identity on a weakening security primitive. *"If it were 2022, I'd be all about it."*

Resolving the original sketch's flagged ambiguity: this is a **positioning move, not a roadmap move.** Verification stays a fully offered capability — it remains RelayKit's only fully horizontal category, working across every vertical without specialization, and it can have its own landing page and SEO targeting its (large) search market. What changes is that the **brand does not rotate around it.** It is a door into RelayKit, not the house.

---

## Call C — marketing: offered, not identity

Symmetric with Call B. Marketing-tier sends stay fully offered — RelayKit wants that business — and marketing can also have its own landing page and SEO for its search market. But marketing is not RelayKit's identity. RelayKit is not a marketing company.

The brand sits *above* both categories. RelayKit empowers builders to add SMS; verification and marketing are two doors in, not the building. This retires the old "two co-equal launch headlines" framing entirely — neither category is the headline.

## The kit decision — Makerkit as RelayKit's own shell (added 2026-06-12)

From a Session 132 working session. Exploration-grade until the pressure-test; the formal commitment is a D-number at the Phase 5/6 boundary.

**RelayKit builds its own production app shell on Makerkit.** The production customer app (real auth, billing, account/settings, authenticated workspace layout) exists today only as `/prototype` (UI spec per D-163) — there is almost nothing to migrate, because the shell a kit provides is exactly what hasn't been built. Makerkit is the pick on technical fit: Supabase-native (Auth + RLS), Next.js 16, Tailwind v4, TS strict, Stripe billing, Turborepo — our exact stack. A backlog scan (2026-06-12) found nothing precluded: multi-user/team access (D-299) and the multi-project dashboard (PRD_11) are accelerated; the platform/multi-tenant tier (PRD_10) is carrier-side architecture no kit touches, though Makerkit's org primitives are a usable chassis for its eventual dashboard surface. First-party plugins cover real future needs (testimonials, feedback widget, beta-gating waitlist, AI-chatbot scaffold, error monitoring). Scope boundary: shell only — `/api`, the SDK, and the registration pipeline stay RelayKit-proprietary. ShipFast is purchased separately for community membership and the Play 1 dogfood integration, not architecture.

**Dogfooding is the second half of the rationale.** Using Makerkit daily makes RelayKit expert members of one kit ecosystem we market into — the same motion we ask of customers, run on ourselves.

**The kit-agnostic core is a named constraint.** The product thesis is that any good AI coding tool (Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf, Codex, Cline) can consistently build RelayKit into any major starter kit (ShipFast, Supastarter, Makerkit, Vercel+Supabase, SaaS Pegasus, and others). Therefore the SDK, AGENTS.md, and core integration artifacts are written general-purpose; per-kit quirks live in thin per-kit guides (Play 2), never in the core. Our own Makerkit adoption is an operations/dogfooding decision, not a positioning one — nothing customer-facing privileges it. This is the firewall against the multi-axis quirk matrix.

**Brownfield posture (resolves tension #1 / question #3):** greenfield first — prove clone-kit → integrate → paid live texts across the kit matrix. Brownfield (existing apps) comes after, offered as "your complexity, our guidance": advice-supported via documentation and integration guidance, not a supported automation path. It is its own curve, entered deliberately once greenfield is proven.

## Open questions for the pressure-test session

1. **Which starter kit is Stage 1?** This is the central bet, not a detail. The original sketch named MakerKit; MASTER_PLAN Phase 9 names KolbySisk and Vercel/Supabase. Lowest-friction + highest-success-probability is the selection criterion. Picking wrong means pounding ground that was never going to hold. *→ Resolved direction (2026-06-12): Makerkit — see "The kit decision" above. Stage-1-kit ≠ first-Play-1-integration ordering (KolbySisk-first sequencing in MARKETING_STRATEGY Play 1 may still hold for validation); pressure-test reconciles.*
2. **How is the golden path messaged?** "Golden path" is internal language. What does the developer-facing story sound like at each stage?
3. **How far, and when, to deal in brownfield?** Its own curve, per the tension above. Needs a deliberate call. *→ Resolved direction (2026-06-12): greenfield first across the kit matrix; brownfield after, advice-supported ("your complexity, our guidance"), never an automated path — see "The kit decision" above.*
4. **What does the pre-launch checklist become?** The current checklist (blog infra, live-site tweaks, configurator refinement, first IH post) was built for a marketing-site-led launch. A starter-kit-led launch front-loads different work: kit selection, integration artifacts (AGENTS.md, per-builder guide), the end-to-end wire-up, a working paid flow. This likely triggers a MASTER_PLAN amendment — but that decision belongs to the pressure-test, not to this exploration.
5. **The aperture-cost tradeoff** — how slow is too slow, given competitors are not gated.
