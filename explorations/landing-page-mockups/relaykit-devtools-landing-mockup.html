How it fits

Who gets the text — and who doesn't.

The five events above are the same on every app. Who should receive them is not — and that's the one decision worth making on purpose.

One owner, one number

For a tool with a single account owner — a solo developer's API key, a one-seat dashboard — routing is trivial. The person who pays is the person you text. A declined card, a trial winding down, a sign-in from an unfamiliar city: all of it goes to the one human attached to the account. Choosing the recipient was never the hard part. Making sure the message arrived was — and that's the gap the numbers above describe.

Many seats, one right recipient

Multi-tenant apps are where it gets interesting. A workspace has a billing owner, a couple of admins, and a stack of members, and the five events don't fan out evenly. A failed payment is the billing owner's problem; texting all twelve seats just makes twelve confused people. A new-device sign-in belongs to exactly one user — the one who signed in. A suspension might need every admin at once. Same event, different human, depending on role.

This is the quiet thing email gets wrong. Billing alerts land in a shared alias nobody owns, or blast the whole org, and the one person who could fix it hears about it from a churned customer instead. A text forces you to name a recipient — which turns out to be exactly the right constraint.

The same five messages, either way

You don't build two systems. Each event is a single call that carries the recipient and the details — the last four digits, the account link, the device label. RelayKit renders the message and sends it. Single-tenant or multi-tenant is just a question of which user you pass; the library doesn't change, and the routing stays yours.

So the work isn't writing the messages or handling delivery — that part's done. It's the one design choice worth your attention: for each event, who needs to know. Get that right, and the account event does its job — reaching the person who can act, somewhere they'll actually see it.
